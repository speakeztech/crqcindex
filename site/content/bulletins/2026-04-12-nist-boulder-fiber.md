## Summary

Researchers at NIST and the University of Colorado Boulder have demonstrated a quantum-resistant fiber optic channel operating at production-relevant speeds. The work builds on lattice-based key encapsulation mechanisms standardized in FIPS 203 (ML-KEM), applied to optical transport layer security.

## Significance

This is notable not for cryptanalytic threat but for **defense readiness**. The demonstration shows that PQC algorithms can operate within the latency budgets of existing fiber infrastructure, removing a key objection to early migration.
