## The Positive Case

Researchers at NIST and the University of Colorado Boulder have reported findings that could reshape assumptions about quantum noise in optical channels. Their work demonstrates that certain noise profiles previously considered obstacles to quantum computation may, paradoxically, provide a substrate for more robust error correction schemes.

This is significant because it suggests the timeline for fault-tolerant quantum computation over distributed fiber networks may be shorter than previously modeled.

## The Threat Case

The convergence of improved error correction, increased qubit counts, and novel architectural approaches creates a compounding effect that linear extrapolation fails to capture. The CRQC Index attempts to model this convergence through the Z estimate, which accounts for correlated advances across multiple fronts.

When multiple independent research programs begin producing results that reinforce each other, the effective timeline compresses non-linearly. This is the core of the convergent conundrum: individual signals may appear incremental, but their intersection accelerates the overall trajectory.

## Implications for Migration Planning

Organizations relying on classical cryptographic primitives should treat convergence signals as leading indicators rather than lagging confirmation. The Mosca inequality (X + Y > Z) becomes increasingly urgent as Z estimates tighten, because Y — migration time — is the variable most within organizational control but least responsive to urgency.
